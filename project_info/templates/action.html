Take Action

So, now you know that Pennsylvania ranks high in the United States for overall CO2 emissions, you know that industrial sectors do way more damage than the residential (and the average person), and you know that some counties (maybe even the one you live in) are home to facilities that are dumping CO2 into Pennsylvania's skies despite warnings that things are getting worse by the year. With the looming and increasing aggression of the climate crisis, it feels automatic to give up. How can I take action when the problem feels so big? What could a possible solution even look like? Well, for starters check out one of the many approaches to this issue with the Thrive Agenda. Like we said before, fights against any form of social injustice are woven together at their roots. Taking care of smaller problems together can result in big change. If it's not for us, it's for the next generation, maybe not our neighbors next door, but our neighbors in time: the people that will live here after we're gone. There are so many groups across the state of Pennsylvania, from Pittsburgh to Philadelphia (the two cities where this rag tag team of data scientists live) and counties in between, fighting for equity at all angles for our state. Start slow, join a local community organization, and take action with them! No one person can change everything we need. It takes a movement! Here is a short list to get you going:
