From 1990-2016, the United States was the sixth greatest producer of carbon emissions in the world, and based on a five-year analysis from 2010-2014, five states (Texas, California, Pennsylvania, Illinois and Ohio) consumed between 1000-2500 million metric tons of carbon (31.5% of national consumption), while the average state consumes around 400 million metric tons.



We decided to more closely examine Pennsylvania (the contributors are Pennsylvania residents) and explore the state’s emissions. From 1990-2016, Pennsylvania’s CO2 emissions per capita were higher than the rest of the United States – why?


In examining total CO2 emissions by sector in 2014, nearly 40.4% of total PA emissions were generated by the Electric Power sector.
